🧠 What is Product Discovery?

Product Discovery is the process of figuring out what to build before you build it. It is where you reduce uncertainty about problems, users, and solutions.

Core Principle: In Discovery, you are not validating your idea — you are discovering if there is a problem worth solving, and if your solution actually works.

🔄 The Big Shift: Delivery vs. Discovery

As a PM, you have probably lived in "Delivery mode." Here is how Discovery is different:

📦 Delivery Mode
• Focus on outputs
• Roadmaps with dates
• "When will it ship?"
• Success = on time
🔍 Discovery Mode
• Focus on outcomes
• Hypotheses to test
• "Should we build this?"
• Success = learning

🎤 Interview Tips for PMs

The Mom Test Rules:
  • Talk about their life, not your idea
  • Ask about specific past experiences, not hypotheticals
  • Listen more than you talk (aim for 80/20)
  • "Tell me more about that" is your best friend

📋 Research Checklist

  • I have a clear research goal
  • I have recruited 5-8 participants
  • I have an interview guide with open-ended questions
  • I have planned how to synthesize findings
  • I have a note-taker or recording setup

📏 Success Metrics vs. Guardrail Metrics

⚠️ Common Trap: Only measuring success metrics. You also need guardrail metrics to ensure you are not breaking things.
✅ Success Metrics
• Task completion rate
• Time to complete
• User satisfaction (CSAT)
• Feature adoption
🛡️ Guardrail Metrics
• Error rates
• Support tickets
• Page load time
• Churn/retention

🎯 Usability Testing in 5 Steps

  1. Define tasks — Give users realistic scenarios, not instructions
  2. Recruit 5 users — You do not need 50; 5 will find 85% of issues
  3. Observe silently — Resist the urge to help!
  4. Note behaviors, not opinions — "They clicked back 3 times" > "They did not like it"
  5. Synthesize patterns — Look for trends, not outliers
